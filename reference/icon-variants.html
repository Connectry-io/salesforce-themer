Connectry Extension Icon — Variant Prototypes

Option 1: Connectry family mark (two dots + line) as the shared identity, with a per-product glyph or treatment layered on top. Testing against a Chrome-toolbar-blue background so we can see which ones read clearly.

Variant A — Monogram inside left node

Left graphite node gets replaced with a letter. Preserves the family silhouette perfectly. Reads at 16px. Recommended.

A1 — Letter "T" (Themer)

Clean sans-serif T, bold, white-on-graphite. Salesforce Themer.

A2 — Letter inside right (accent) node

Monogram on the colored node instead. Feels more "brand → product."

A3 — Product accent color

Family mark identical; only the accent-node color changes per product. Themer = blue, VAT Checker = teal, etc.

Variant B — Node becomes the glyph

The accent node morphs into a product-specific shape (paint drop, checkmark, etc). Family is the two-shapes-connected motif, product is the right-hand shape.

B1 — Paint drop (Themer)

Right node becomes a paint droplet. Clearly signals "theming" even at tiny sizes.

B2 — Color palette swatches

Right node replaced by a stack of three mini color swatches. Very "theming" but busier at 16px.

B3 — Salesforce cloud silhouette

Right node becomes a tiny cloud silhouette. Very literal "this is for Salesforce." Risk: looks like SF's icon, trademark grey area.

Variant C — Badge overlay

Keep the family mark exactly as-is, and tuck a small product glyph as a corner badge. The original logo stays untouched.

C1 — "T" badge, bottom-right

Family mark unchanged. Small white badge in the corner with product letter.

C2 — Paint drop badge

Family mark unchanged. Tiny paint-drop badge in the corner. Keeps Connectry identity pristine.

C3 — Connectry mark on product tile

Inverts the hierarchy: the icon IS the product glyph (paint drop here), and Connectry mark sits as the corner badge. Product-first branding.
